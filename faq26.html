How Do I Track Stock Splits?

Stock splits are common corporate actions that can change the number of shares you own without changing the overall value of your investment. While they may appear confusing at first, tracking stock splits becomes much easier when you understand how they work and use the right tools.

Whether you invest in a few companies or maintain a diversified portfolio, keeping accurate records after stock splits helps preserve the integrity of your investment history and performance analysis.

What Is a Stock Split?

A stock split occurs when a company increases the number of outstanding shares by dividing existing shares according to a predetermined ratio. Although the number of shares changes, the total market value of the investment immediately after the split generally remains the same.

For example, in a 2-for-1 stock split, investors receive two shares for every one share previously owned. The share price adjusts proportionally, while the overall value of the position remains unchanged.

Common examples of stock splits include:
  • 2-for-1 stock split.
  • 3-for-1 stock split.
  • 3-for-2 stock split.
  • 4-for-1 stock split.
  • 5-for-1 stock split.
  • 10-for-1 stock split.
  • Other company-specific split ratios.

Companies may choose to split their shares for a variety of reasons, often related to maintaining a share price range they believe is attractive to investors.
