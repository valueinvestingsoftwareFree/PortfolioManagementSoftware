Why Importing Transactions Matters

Every investment decision creates a record. Purchases, sales, dividends, deposits, withdrawals, stock splits, and other activities all contribute to your portfolio's story. Without organized records, understanding how your investments evolved over time can become increasingly difficult.

Importing transactions helps preserve that history while reducing repetitive manual work and minimizing the likelihood of data entry errors.

Importing investment transactions can help investors:
  • Save time when setting up portfolios.
  • Maintain complete transaction histories.
  • Reduce manual entry errors.
  • Track cost basis information accurately.
  • Monitor realized and unrealized gains.
  • Review dividend activity.
  • Evaluate long-term portfolio performance.
  • Understand allocation changes over time.
  • Support performance analysis.
  • Improve overall organization.

Whether importing a few months of records or many years of investment history, having complete information available in one place can significantly improve portfolio management and decision-making.
