What Should Be Included in Your Investment History?

A complete investment history is much more than a list of stock purchases. It should provide enough information to reconstruct every important event that has affected your portfolio throughout the years.

Keeping comprehensive records allows investors to evaluate not only investment returns but also the decisions behind those returns. The more complete your historical data, the more meaningful your future analysis becomes.

Rather than relying on memory or scattered spreadsheets, maintaining a structured historical database creates a reliable foundation for long-term investing.

Essential Information to Track

🛒 Purchase Transactions

Record purchase dates, quantities, prices, commissions, and total investment cost.

💵 Sale Transactions

Track selling prices, dates, commissions, realized gains, and realized losses.

💰 Dividend Payments

Maintain a complete history of cash dividends and stock dividends received.

📈 Portfolio Valuation

Monitor market value, intrinsic value estimates, and historical valuation changes.

🏢 Company Information

Store business quality metrics, sector classification, rankings, and investment notes.

📊 Performance Metrics

Review historical returns, income generation, allocation changes, and long-term growth.

Building a Valuable Long-Term Database

One of the greatest advantages of maintaining investment history is that its value increases every year. After several years of investing, your database becomes an invaluable resource for understanding how your investment philosophy performs under different market conditions.

Historical records reveal patterns that would otherwise remain hidden. They help identify which industries consistently perform well, how dividend income evolves, which valuation methods work best, and how individual companies behave throughout economic cycles.

This accumulated knowledge becomes a powerful competitive advantage for disciplined investors who continuously learn from their own investment experience.
