What Are Capital Gains?

Capital gains generally refer to the increase in value of an investment relative to its original purchase price. They can occur when investments appreciate over time, and they are commonly separated into two categories: realized gains and unrealized gains.

Understanding the distinction between these two concepts helps investors interpret portfolio performance more accurately and maintain organized records of investment activity.

Capital gain concepts include:
  • Realized gains: Profits generated from investments that have been sold.
  • Unrealized gains: Increases in value associated with investments that are still being held.
  • Cost basis: The original value used to evaluate gains or losses.
  • Total return: Performance that may include both gains and investment income.
  • Historical records: Documentation of transactions supporting performance reviews.

By monitoring these components consistently, investors can gain a clearer understanding of how individual investments contribute to overall portfolio results.

Reviewing both measurements regularly helps investors maintain perspective and gain a more complete understanding of their portfolio's evolution.
