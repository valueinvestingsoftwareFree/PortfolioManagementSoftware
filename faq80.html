Can I Track Preferred Stocks?

Yes. Preferred stocks can and should be tracked just like common stocks, especially if they represent an important source of dividend income within your investment portfolio. Monitoring preferred shares allows investors to organize purchases, evaluate dividend income, follow historical performance, review portfolio allocation, and better understand how these investments contribute to long-term financial goals.

Why Track Preferred Stocks?

Preferred stocks occupy a unique position between common stocks and bonds. They often provide predictable dividend payments while typically experiencing lower price volatility than common shares. For income-focused investors, retirees, and long-term value investors, preferred stocks can become an important component of a diversified portfolio.

Keeping detailed records of preferred stock purchases, dividend payments, acquisition prices, income generated, and portfolio allocation makes it much easier to evaluate whether your investment strategy continues to meet your financial objectives.
