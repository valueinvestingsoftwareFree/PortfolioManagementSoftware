How Do I Calculate Investment Income?

Investment income represents the money generated by your investments without requiring you to sell your assets. For many investors, understanding how to calculate investment income is essential for evaluating portfolio performance, planning retirement, and building long-term financial security.

Whether your income comes from dividends, bond interest, ETF distributions, or other recurring payments, accurate tracking helps you understand how effectively your investments support your financial goals.

What Is Investment Income?

Investment income refers to the earnings generated by assets you own. Unlike capital gains, which occur when investments are sold for more than their purchase price, investment income is typically received while continuing to hold the underlying assets.

For investors seeking financial independence or retirement income, these recurring earnings can become an important source of cash flow.

Common sources of investment income include:
  • Dividend payments from stocks.
  • Bond interest payments.
  • ETF distributions.
  • Mutual fund distributions.
  • Interest earned on cash positions.
  • REIT distributions.
  • International dividend income.
  • Other recurring investment payments.

Identifying all sources of income ensures that investors gain an accurate understanding of how much their portfolios generate over time.

How Is Investment Income Calculated?

Calculating investment income generally involves adding together all income-producing payments received during a specified period, such as monthly, quarterly, or annually.

A simple investment income calculation may include:
  • Total dividends received.
  • Total bond interest earned.
  • ETF and mutual fund distributions.
  • Cash interest payments.
  • Other recurring investment earnings.

For example, if an investor receives $2,000 in dividends, $500 in bond interest, and $300 from ETF distributions during a year, total investment income would equal $2,800.

Tracking these amounts consistently provides valuable insight into the growth and reliability of passive income streams.
