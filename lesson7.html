Technical Analysis: RSI, Moving Averages and Bollinger Bands

Technical analysis is a widely used method for evaluating stocks by studying historical price movements and trading patterns. Instead of focusing only on financial statements, technical analysis uses charts and indicators to help investors identify trends, momentum, and potential entry or exit points.

Among the most commonly used indicators are the Relative Strength Index (RSI), Moving Averages, and Bollinger Bands. These tools help investors better understand market behavior and price dynamics.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum indicator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. It typically ranges from 0 to 100.

  • An RSI above 70 may indicate that a stock is overbought.
  • An RSI below 30 may suggest that the stock is oversold.

Investors often use RSI to identify potential turning points in the market, especially when combined with other indicators.

Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth price data over a specific period, helping investors identify the underlying trend of a stock.

Two of the most common types are:

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA) – Calculates the average price over a fixed period.
  • Exponential Moving Average (EMA) – Gives more weight to recent prices.

Moving averages are frequently used to detect trend direction. When the price moves above or below a moving average, it may signal potential changes in market momentum.

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands are a volatility indicator that consists of three lines:

  • A middle line representing a moving average.
  • An upper band representing a higher volatility range.
  • A lower band representing a lower volatility range.

When prices move near the upper band, the asset may be considered relatively expensive. When prices approach the lower band, the asset may appear relatively cheap.

Bollinger Bands are especially useful for identifying periods of high or low market volatility.

Combining Indicators for Better Insights

No single technical indicator should be used in isolation. Many investors combine RSI, moving averages, and Bollinger Bands to obtain a more complete view of market conditions.

For example:

  • RSI can highlight potential overbought or oversold conditions.
  • Moving averages reveal the overall market trend.
  • Bollinger Bands show price volatility and potential breakout points.

Together, these indicators help investors make more informed decisions when analyzing stock price behavior.
