What Metrics Matter Most?

The most effective mutual fund tracking systems focus on multiple measurements rather than relying exclusively on current values.

Important mutual fund metrics include:
  • Cost Basis: The original amount invested.
  • Current Value: The latest portfolio valuation.
  • Total Return: Appreciation plus reinvested distributions.
  • Dividend Income: Cash distributions received.
  • Unrealized Gains: Potential profits on existing holdings.
  • Realized Gains: Profits generated from completed sales.
  • Transaction History: Contributions and withdrawals.
  • Portfolio Weight: The percentage allocated to each fund.
  • Investment Timeline: Historical evolution over time.

Monitoring these metrics together provides meaningful insights into the health and effectiveness of an investment strategy.

It also helps investors identify trends and understand how consistent contributions affect long-term outcomes.
