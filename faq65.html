How Do I Calculate Dividend Yield?

Dividend yield is one of the most widely used metrics among income-focused investors. It helps measure how much annual dividend income an investment generates relative to its current market price. Understanding dividend yield allows investors to compare income opportunities and evaluate whether a dividend-paying investment aligns with their financial goals.

Whether you invest in individual stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, or build an entire dividend portfolio, knowing how to calculate and monitor dividend yield can improve decision-making and help you better understand the income potential of your investments.

What Is Dividend Yield?

Dividend yield measures the percentage of income an investor receives from dividends compared to the current market value of an investment. It provides a quick way to estimate how much cash flow an investment may generate annually.

The dividend yield formula is:

Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend Per Share ÷ Current Share Price) × 100

For example, if a stock pays annual dividends of $4 per share and its current market price is $100 per share:

  1. Annual dividend per share: $4
  2. Current share price: $100
  3. $4 ÷ $100 = 0.04
  4. 0.04 × 100 = 4%

In this example, the stock has a dividend yield of 4%.

Dividend Yield Versus Yield on Cost

Many investors confuse dividend yield with yield on cost. Although related, these metrics measure different aspects of investment income.

Dividend Yield

Uses the current market price to estimate present income generation.

Yield on Cost

Uses the original purchase price to measure income growth over time.

Current Analysis

Dividend yield helps compare today's opportunities.

Historical Perspective

Yield on cost highlights long-term dividend growth success.

Both metrics provide valuable insights and are often used together by dividend investors.
