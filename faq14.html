One of the most common questions among both beginner and experienced investors is:

Can I track ETFs?

The answer is absolutely yes.

Exchange-Traded Funds, commonly known as ETFs, have become one of the most popular investment vehicles in the world. Investors appreciate them because they offer diversification, simplicity, and exposure to entire markets, sectors, countries, or investment strategies.

However, buying ETFs is only part of the investing journey. Understanding how those ETFs perform over time is equally important.

Tracking ETFs allows investors to monitor growth, evaluate investment decisions, understand risks, and determine whether their portfolios remain aligned with their financial goals.

ETF tracking generally involves monitoring:
  • Current market value.
  • Cost basis.
  • Total return.
  • Dividend income.
  • Unrealized gains.
  • Realized gains.
  • Historical transactions.
  • Portfolio allocation.

Without proper tracking, it becomes difficult to understand how well your ETF strategy is actually performing.

What Information Should You Monitor?

ETF tracking goes beyond simply checking today's market price.

To truly understand investment performance, investors should monitor several important metrics.

Key ETF Metrics Include:
  • Cost Basis: The amount originally invested.
  • Current Value: The present market value.
  • Total Return: Growth plus dividends received.
  • Dividend History: Income distributions over time.
  • Unrealized Gains: Potential profits on current holdings.
  • Realized Gains: Profits from completed sales.
  • Transaction History: Purchases and sales.
  • Portfolio Weight: The percentage each ETF represents.

Monitoring these measurements together provides a much clearer picture of portfolio health than market prices alone.

This comprehensive perspective helps investors maintain discipline during periods of market volatility.
