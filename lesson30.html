CAGR Purchase by Purchase

The Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) is one of the most useful metrics for evaluating the performance of stock investments. Calculating CAGR purchase by purchase allows investors to analyze the return generated by each individual stock transaction.

Instead of measuring performance only at the portfolio level, this method evaluates how each purchase contributes to the overall investment results.

Understanding CAGR

CAGR represents the average annual growth rate of an investment over a specific period of time. It smooths out fluctuations and expresses performance as a steady annual return.

This metric helps investors compare investments with different holding periods and evaluate long-term profitability.

Why Analyze CAGR Per Purchase

Many investors buy the same stock multiple times at different prices and dates. Calculating CAGR for each purchase separately provides a deeper understanding of investment timing and decision quality.

This approach helps identify which transactions have generated the highest returns and which ones have performed below expectations.

Factors Affecting CAGR

Several factors influence the CAGR of a stock investment:

  • Purchase price
  • Current market price
  • Holding period
  • Dividends received
  • Transaction costs

Accurate records of each purchase allow investors to calculate reliable CAGR values.

Improving Investment Strategies

By reviewing CAGR results purchase by purchase, investors can learn from past investment decisions and improve future strategies.

This analysis helps identify successful entry points, strong long-term holdings, and potential weaknesses in the investment process.
