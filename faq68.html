Why Investors Spend So Much Time Managing Portfolios

Many investors begin their journey using spreadsheets and brokerage statements to track their investments. While this approach can work initially, it often becomes increasingly difficult as portfolios grow and investment strategies become more sophisticated.

Recording transactions manually, updating balances, calculating dividend income, and reviewing historical performance can consume valuable hours that could otherwise be spent researching opportunities or enjoying life outside investing.

Common time-consuming investment tasks include:
  • Updating spreadsheets after every transaction.
  • Calculating portfolio performance manually.
  • Tracking dividend payments.
  • Reviewing historical purchases and sales.
  • Organizing brokerage statements.
  • Calculating realized gains and losses.
  • Monitoring unrealized gains.
  • Reviewing asset allocation percentages.
  • Comparing multiple investment accounts.
  • Preparing information for future analysis.

As the number of holdings increases, these repetitive tasks can become overwhelming. Portfolio software helps eliminate much of this administrative burden by organizing information automatically and presenting it in a structured format.
